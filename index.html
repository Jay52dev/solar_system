Mercury

The smallest and innermost planet in the Solar System, Mercury's proximity to the Sun makes it one of the hottest worlds in our celestial neighborhood.

  • Diameter: 4,879 km
  • Day Length: 176 Earth days
  • Year Length: 88 Earth days

Venus

Often called Earth's sister planet due to its similar size, Venus has a toxic atmosphere and is the hottest planet in our Solar System.

  • Diameter: 12,104 km
  • Day Length: 243 Earth days
  • Year Length: 225 Earth days

Earth

Our home planet is the only world known to harbor life. It's the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbor life.

  • Diameter: 12,742 km
  • Day Length: 24 hours
  • Year Length: 365.25 days

Mars

Known as the Red Planet, Mars is a terrestrial planet with a thin atmosphere and surface features reminiscent of the Moon's craters.

  • Diameter: 6,779 km
  • Day Length: 24.6 hours
  • Year Length: 687 Earth days

Jupiter

The largest planet in our Solar System, Jupiter is a gas giant with a stunning pattern of swirling clouds and the famous Great Red Spot.

  • Diameter: 139,820 km
  • Day Length: 9.93 hours
  • Year Length: 11.86 Earth years

Saturn

Famous for its spectacular ring system, Saturn is a gas giant with an extensive system of moons, including Titan, the second-largest in the Solar System.

  • Diameter: 116,460 km
  • Day Length: 10.7 hours
  • Year Length: 29.46 Earth years

Uranus

The ice giant Uranus has a unique sideways rotation and a pale blue color due to its hydrogen and helium atmosphere with methane.

  • Diameter: 50,724 km
  • Day Length: 17.24 hours
  • Year Length: 84 Earth years

Neptune

The farthest known planet from the Sun, Neptune is a dark, cold world with supersonic winds and the Great Dark Spot, a storm similar to Jupiter's Great Red Spot.

  • Diameter: 49,244 km
  • Day Length: 16.11 hours
  • Year Length: 165 Earth years

Discover Distant Galaxies

Milky Way

Our home galaxy is a barred spiral galaxy containing our Solar System and billions of other stars, planets, and cosmic objects.

  • Type: Barred Spiral
  • Diameter: 100,000 light-years
  • Age: 13.6 billion years

Andromeda

Andromeda is the nearest spiral galaxy to our Milky Way and is on a collision course with our galaxy, expected to merge in about 4.5 billion years.

  • Type: Spiral
  • Distance: 2.537 million light-years
  • Diameter: 220,000 light-years

Triangulum

The Triangulum Galaxy is the third-largest member of the Local Group of galaxies, which includes the Milky Way and Andromeda.

  • Type: Spiral
  • Distance: 2.73 million light-years
  • Diameter: 60,000 light-years

Fascinating Space Facts

01

Space is Completely Silent

In space, there is no atmosphere or medium for sound waves to travel through. This means that space is completely silent, despite the cosmic events happening all around.

02

The Sun is Enormous

The Sun accounts for 99.86% of the mass in our solar system. It would take about 1.3 million Earths to fill up the Sun if it were hollow.

03

One Day on Venus is Longer Than One Year

Venus rotates so slowly that it takes 243 Earth days to complete one rotation. However, it only takes 225 Earth days to complete one orbit around the Sun.

04

There are More Stars Than Grains of Sand on Earth

Astronomers estimate there are about 10 sextillion stars in the observable universe. That's more than all the grains of sand on all the beaches on Earth.

05

Black Holes Distort Time

Black holes have such strong gravity that they warp the fabric of space-time. Time passes more slowly near a black hole than it does farther away.

06

The Great Red Spot is Shrinking

Jupiter's Great Red Spot, a giant storm that has been raging for at least 400 years, is gradually shrinking. It was once three times the size of Earth.
