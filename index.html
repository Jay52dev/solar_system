Mercury

The smallest and innermost planet in our Solar System, known for its cratered surface.

  • Distance: 58 million km from Sun
  • Diameter: 4,880 km
  • Day length: 59 Earth days

Venus

Known as Earth's "sister planet" due to similar size, Venus has a toxic atmosphere and extreme heat.

  • Distance: 108 million km from Sun
  • Diameter: 12,104 km
  • Day length: 243 Earth days

Earth

Our home planet, the only known world with liquid water and life on its surface.

  • Distance: 150 million km from Sun
  • Diameter: 12,742 km
  • Day length: 24 hours

Mars

The "Red Planet" with polar ice caps and the largest volcano in our Solar System.

  • Distance: 228 million km from Sun
  • Diameter: 6,779 km
  • Day length: 24.6 hours

Jupiter

The largest planet in our Solar System, famous for its Great Red Spot storm.

  • Distance: 778 million km from Sun
  • Diameter: 139,820 km
  • Day length: 9.93 hours

Saturn

Known for its spectacular ring system made of ice particles and rocky debris.

  • Distance: 1.4 billion km from Sun
  • Diameter: 116,460 km
  • Day length: 10.7 hours

Uranus

The "sideways planet" that rotates on its side with a unique blue-green color.

  • Distance: 2.9 billion km from Sun
  • Diameter: 50,724 km
  • Day length: 17.2 hours

Neptune

The windiest planet with the strongest winds in the Solar System reaching 2,100 km/h.

  • Distance: 4.5 billion km from Sun
  • Diameter: 49,244 km
  • Day length: 16.1 hours

Fascinating Galaxies

Milky Way

Our home galaxy containing our solar system among billions of other stars.

  • Type: Barred Spiral Galaxy
  • Diameter: 100,000 light years
  • Stars: 100-400 billion

Andromeda

The closest major galaxy to the Milky Way and the largest in our Local Group.

  • Type: Spiral Galaxy
  • Distance: 2.5 million light years
  • Stars: 1 trillion

Triangulum

The third-largest member of the Local Group of galaxies.

  • Type: Spiral Galaxy
  • Distance: 2.7 million light years
  • Diameter: 60,000 light years

Amazing Space Facts

01

Space is Completely Silent

In space, there is no atmosphere or medium for sound waves to travel through, making it completely silent regardless of all the violent, explosive events happening.

02

The Sun's Mass

The Sun accounts for 99.86% of the mass in the solar system. The gas giants Jupiter and Saturn account for most of the remaining 0.14%.

03

Neutron Stars

Neutron stars are so dense that a teaspoon of their material would weigh about a billion tons on Earth.

04

Space Smells

Astronauts report that space smells like hot metal, seared steak, and welding fumes due to the presence of polyatomic chemical species.

05

Venus Day

A day on Venus (243 Earth days) is longer than a year on Venus (225 Earth days).

06

Black Holes

Black holes aren't empty spaces but are actually the most dense objects in our universe, with such strong gravity that nothing can escape them.
